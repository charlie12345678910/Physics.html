Description

An electronic doorbell has an inbuilt circutiry which produce a high monotone sound that acts as the sound for the doorbell when the switch is pressed.

The purpose of this website is to describe the mechanism of an electronic doorbell. The electronic components used for this experimentation were transistors, speakers, jumper wires, breadboard, capacitors, switch and an amplifier circuit.
This doorbell used the concept of RC circuit using an Ac source When the switch was closed, a sound is produced from the speaker as a result of the frequencies in the circuit and it served as an electronic doorbell.The work of the transistor was to amplify the current passing through the circuit by fluctuating the electrical pulses or currents.Thus, sound could be heard. The purpose of the resistor was to also help in the gradual charging and discharging of current in the capacitors as they stored charges. This generated frequency in the current and it was amplified by the transistors. When we connected our circuit to a DC source, it produced a sound with a low tone so in order to get a clearer sound we connected an Ac source and varied the frequencies across it.
